Places in the Bucket List

Tromsø

Situated in Norway, Tromsø is largely regarded to be the best place to watch Aurora Borealis, better known as the Northern Lights. These pillars of light are caused by the charged particles emitted from the sun whose interaction with the Earth's atmosphere causes emissions in the visible light spectrum. Moreover, Norway is also popular for the following:

  • Bergen: A picturesque coastal city with colorful houses along the waterfront
  • Jotunheimen National Park: Norway's largest national park with numerous trails for hiking
  • Oslo: Norway's capital; houses the Viking Ship Museum, the Munch Museum, and the Royal Palace

Reykjavik

Iceland's capital city, popular for its geothermal energy, museums and architectural delights. Being a country of high volcanic activity, Iceland's shores are lined with basalt columns and beaches made of volcanic rocks, giving the illusion of a "Black Beach".

  • Geothermal Fissures, Waterfalls and Hotsprings
  • Vast Glacial Landscapes
  • Unique Tundra Wildlife

Sof Omar

Carved out of limestone by Weyib River, Sof Omar is one of the world's largest cave systems. The caves are of great historical and religious significance to the local population and has served as one of the most popular tourist destinations in Ethiopia.

  • Chamber of Columns
  • Annual Festival that draws pilgrims and visitors from different parts of the country

Places I've Visited

Arba Minch

Located in the south western region of Ethiopia, Arba Minch is a town with plenty gifts from mother nature. The most popular one being a thin strip of land separating two lakes, locally known as "The Bridge of God". The local populus enjoys freshly harvested cereals and crops along with a fishery industry supported by two big lakes. Moreover, the area is popular for the abundance of crocodiles and crocodile breeding facilities.

Teklehaiymanot Monastery

Overshadowed by the nearby Debre Libanos Monastery, Teklehaiymanot sits a few kilometers away from Fiche. The abscence of any roads forces travellers to go on a three-hour hike for a glance at the monastery, half of which is embedded on the side of a mountain.
